CONSTRUCTION MARKETPLACE — INTERNAL FAQ & POLICIES
====================================================

SECTION 1: PROJECT DELAYS
--------------------------
Q: What factors most commonly cause construction project delays?

A: Construction project delays typically arise from the following key factors:

1. Weather Conditions: Extreme weather such as heavy rain, snow, or high winds can halt outdoor work.
   Projects in monsoon-prone or cold regions must build buffer time into schedules.

2. Supply Chain Disruptions: Shortages of materials like steel, concrete, lumber, or specialized
   equipment can stall progress. Post-pandemic supply chain issues have extended lead times by
   4–12 weeks for many critical materials.

3. Labour Shortages: Skilled tradesperson shortages (electricians, plumbers, structural engineers)
   are a growing issue. Contractors should maintain pre-qualified subcontractor lists.

4. Design Changes: Late-stage design revisions by clients add rework costs and delay approvals.
   Change orders must be formally documented and signed before work proceeds.

5. Permit and Regulatory Approvals: Delays in obtaining building permits, environmental clearances,
   or inspections from municipal bodies are a leading cause of schedule slippage.

6. Site Conditions: Unexpected underground conditions such as rock formations, old foundations,
   or contaminated soil require remediation before construction can resume.

7. Subcontractor Performance: Poor coordination between subcontractors, or a sub failing to
   mobilise on time, creates cascade delays across dependent trades.

8. Financing Issues: Delayed payment from project owners or financing gaps can cause work stoppages.


SECTION 2: SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
-------------------------------
All contractors operating on the marketplace platform must comply with the following safety standards:

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):
- Hard hats (Class E) are mandatory on all active construction sites.
- High-visibility vests are required in areas with vehicle movement.
- Steel-toed boots and gloves are required for all ground workers.
- Harnesses and fall-arrest systems are mandatory for any work above 2 metres.

Site Safety Plan:
- Every project must have a written Site Safety Plan submitted before work begins.
- The plan must identify hazards, mitigation measures, and emergency contacts.
- A designated Safety Officer must be named for projects with more than 10 workers.

Incident Reporting:
- All injuries must be reported within 24 hours using the Incident Report Form.
- Fatalities or serious injuries must be reported to the relevant regulatory authority
  within 1 hour of occurrence.
- Contractors with 3 or more incidents in 12 months will be suspended pending review.


SECTION 3: CONTRACTOR PAYMENT POLICIES
---------------------------------------
Payment Schedule:
- Payments are milestone-based: 20% on mobilisation, 40% at structural completion,
  30% at practical completion, 10% on final handover.
- Invoices must be submitted through the platform portal by the 25th of each month.
- Payment is processed within 14 business days of invoice approval.

Disputes:
- Payment disputes must be raised in writing within 7 days of receiving a disputed invoice.
- A neutral arbitrator will be assigned if the dispute is not resolved within 21 days.

Retention:
- A 5% retention is held until the defects liability period (typically 12 months) expires.
- Retention is released within 30 days of the defects liability period end date.


SECTION 4: TENDERING AND BID REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------
Eligibility:
- All contractors must hold a valid trade licence for the category of work being tendered.
- A minimum of 3 years trading history and 2 reference projects are required.
- Contractors must carry public liability insurance of at least $5 million.

Bid Submission:
- Bids must be submitted at least 5 business days before the tender closing date.
- A Bid Bond of 2% of the project value is required for projects over $500,000.
- Late bids will not be accepted under any circumstances.

Evaluation Criteria:
- Price: 50%, Technical capability: 30%, Safety record: 20%.


SECTION 5: QUALITY STANDARDS
-----------------------------
Material Standards:
- All structural concrete must meet a minimum compressive strength of 25 MPa.
- Steel reinforcement must comply with AS 4671 or equivalent international standard.
- Imported materials must carry certification from the country of origin.

Inspection Milestones:
- Foundation inspection: before pouring concrete.
- Framing inspection: before installing insulation or cladding.
- Final inspection: before handover to the client.

Defects Liability:
- Contractors are responsible for rectifying defects reported within the 12-month
  defects liability period at no additional cost to the client.
- Failure to rectify within 14 days of notification entitles the client to engage
  another contractor and deduct costs from the retention.
